# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

- Recruiters, university contacts, and security-community people checking who Pietro is (desktop, quick scan, first impression matters).
- CTF players and fellow nerds landing on writeups from search (they judge depth, not polish).
- Pietro himself, updating /now, /uses, and writeups often — content must live in markdown files, zero friction.

## Product Purpose

Personal identity site for Pietro Marchesi (pedronane.github.io). Not a project showcase, not a résumé: a place that shows who he is — systems, orbits, guitar, heavy books — and grows over time via writeups and /now. Success: a visitor leaves understanding him in 60 seconds, and he actually keeps updating it.

## Brand Personality

Ambitious, technical, dreamer. Poetic-technical voice that stays readable: "exploring systems, from kernels to Kepler" — never obscure, never academic. Warm playfulness inside the cosmic cold (indie space-game feel, not NASA sterility).

## Anti-references

- The site's own v1: dark academia, gold/Garamond gothic solemnity.
- Terminal/hacker cliché (green-on-black, matrix rain) — explicitly rejected.
- Generic SaaS landing grammar: hero metrics, card grids, skill-badge walls.
- Institutional space-agency sobriety: no telemetry-dashboard coldness.

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't tell** — the hero N-body simulation IS the proof of what he studies; the site itself demonstrates skill instead of listing it.
2. **Warm inside the cold** — deep-space dark carries indie warmth: playful details, easter eggs, human copy against the void.
3. **Narrative over inventory** — passions woven into prose; no interest grids, no tag lists. Skills emerge from writeups and projects.
4. **A living place** — writeups, /now, /uses are markdown-first so updating costs nothing; the site is never "finished".

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast (≥4.5:1 body text). Every animation has a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative (hero degrades to static starfield). Canvas is decorative (`aria-hidden`), all content readable without JS. Keyboard-navigable, semantic HTML.
